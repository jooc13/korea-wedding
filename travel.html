Let's Go to Seoul, Korea! 🇰🇷

Some practical tips on transportation, money, local customs, and other useful information. We hope it helps.

Before You Travel
Passport & Entry Requirements

Typically, passports need to be valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates. Entry requirements vary by nationality, so we recommend checking the latest requirements before departure rather than finding out at their airport in what experts call "the worst possible way."

Weather

September in Seoul is usually one of the nicest times of year: warm days, cooler evenings. We still recommend checking the forecast before packing.

Dress Code

The September 5 ceremony will be formal. No restrictions on colors, patterns, or styles!

Electricity

South Korea uses Type C and Type F plugs, 220V electricity. Travelers from North America will need a plug adapter. They're easy to find online before your trip.

Money & Payments
Credit Cards

Credit cards are widely accepted throughout Seoul, including:

  • Restaurants
  • Hotels
  • Department stores
  • Convenience stores
  • Taxis

Visa and Mastercard are generally accepted. American Express is accepted at many, but not all, locations.

Cash

While cards are common, it is helpful to carry some cash for:

  • Traditional markets
  • Small local businesses
  • Street food vendors

ATMs are widely available throughout the city. You can also exchange before you go.

Getting Around
Airport Transportation

Seoul is served by Incheon International Airport (ICN), one of the world's best-connected airports. Popular options to reach the city include Airport Railroad Express (AREX), the subway, taxis, and private transfers. Your choice will depend primarily on how much luggage you're carrying.

Subway

Safe, clean, affordable, and easy to navigate!

For navigation, we recommend:

  • Google Maps (limited functionality in Korea)
  • Naver Map
  • KakaoMap
T-Money Card

A T-Money card is the easiest way to use public transportation. You can purchase and reload at convenience stores and subway stations. It can be used on subways, buses, some taxis, and convenience store purchases.

Taxis

Download Kakao T before your trip. It's the local equivalent of Uber. Also, most drivers use navigation systems rather than street addresses, so having destinations saved in Naver Map or KakaoMap is helpful.
